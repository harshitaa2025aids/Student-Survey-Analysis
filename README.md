# Student-Survey-Analysis
##Survey data analysis using Excel
##Student Career Readiness Survey Analysis
###Objective
This survey was conducted to understand students’ current year of study, primary career goals, confidence levels, skill development habits, and challenges faced in skill development.
###Data Collection
-Total Responses: 32
-Method: Google Forms
-Participants: College students from various years
###Key Findings
##Current Year of Study
-71.9% of respondents are first-year students.
-25% are second-year students.
-1% are fourth-year students.
-This indicates the survey mostly represents early-year students.
##Primary Career Goal
-50% aim for corporate jobs.
-31.3% are interested in startups or entrepreneurship.
-12.5% want to pursue higher studies.
-6.3% plan for government jobs.
-Corporate careers dominate, with a notable interest in entrepreneurship.
##Confidence in Achieving Career Goals
-3.1% rated their confidence as 1 (very low).
-12.5% rated 2.
-34.4% rated 3.
-21.9% rated 4.
-28.1% rated 5 (very confident).
-Most students have moderate to high confidence, but a few have low confidence.
##Hours Spent on Skill Development per Week
-43.8% spend 2 hours weekly.
-21.9% spend 1 hour.
-15.6% spend 3–5 hours.
-6.3% spend 6–10 hours.
-6.3% spend more than 10 hours.
-6.3% spend 0 hours.
-Majority spend 1–2 hours weekly, showing scope for increased consistency.
###Workshop or Internship Attendance
-62.5% have not attended any workshop or internship.
-37.5% have participated in workshops or internships.
-This highlights the need for more exposure to practical experiences.
###Skills Being Developed
-Students reported developing the following skills:
-AI, coding skills, DSA, MATLAB, CAD, programming, full-stack web, design skills, basic computers, Python backend, technical skills, aptitude, app development, and some reported nothing.
-Indicates a wide range of skills, but some students are not currently developing any.
###Biggest Obstacle in Skill Development
-40.6% cited lack of consistency.
-25% cited lack of guidance.
-25% cited lack of time.
-6.3% cited not knowing where to start.
-3.1% cited financial constraints.
-Lack of consistency is the primary challenge, followed by guidance and time management issues.
###Insights
-Students are ambitious, with corporate careers being most popular, followed by entrepreneurship.
-Confidence levels are mostly moderate to high.
-Skill development hours are low, showing room for structured practice.
-Workshop and internship participation is limited.
-Main obstacles: consistency, guidance, and time management.
Conclusion
The survey suggests that while students are motivated and career-oriented, they need structured practice, mentorship, and guidance to improve skill development and achieve their career goals.
